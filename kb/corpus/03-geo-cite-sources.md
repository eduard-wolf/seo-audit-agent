# GEO: Cite Sources for Generative Engine Optimisation

## What GEO Means

Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO) is the practice of making web content more likely to be cited, quoted, or summarised by AI-powered search engines and large language models (LLMs). Unlike classic SEO, GEO does not target a ranked list of blue links — it targets inclusion in AI-generated answers.

## Why Citations Matter

When an LLM cites your page, it signals to users that your content is a credible, authoritative source. High citation frequency can drive direct traffic even when the traditional rank position is low.

## How to Cite Sources in Your Own Content

Citing third-party sources in your writing:
1. **Builds E-E-A-T signals** (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness).
2. **Increases the chance that your content is quoted** — LLMs tend to prefer content that itself demonstrates careful sourcing.
3. **Reduces hallucination risk** when your page is used as context for AI inference.

Use `<cite>` tags and descriptive anchor text for in-text citations. Link to primary sources (studies, official documentation, government data) rather than to aggregators.

## Structured Citations

Add `citation` properties inside Schema.org types such as `Article`, `ScholarlyArticle`, or `ClaimReview`. This makes citations machine-readable.

## Authoritative Domain Signals

Mentioning or linking to authoritative domains (academic journals, official bodies, well-known news outlets) increases the semantic trust score of your content in retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) pipelines.
